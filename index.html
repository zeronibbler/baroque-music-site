Welcome to Baroque Music

This site offers an introduction to Baroque music for conservatory students. Baroque, a Portuguese word meaning an oddly shaped pearl, is a term used to describe European music from about 1600 to 1750. While Baroque was initially considered an odd and exaggerated form due to it's intensities and musical decorations, has indeed inspired throughout the course of history numerous musicians and themes. Baroque has been an expressive and bold music that has reformed the history of music. This website is dedicated in the drafting a basic outline of the historical context in which baroque came, the important characteristics of Baroque music, the instruments of the era and, finally, the composers that we ought to remember and that are associated with the era.

We will discover the rich world of Baroque music, its composers, and the instruments that shaped Western music. Basic historical facts, paint the background for listening the Baroque masterpieces, and give us a new perspective on how we understand music today.
